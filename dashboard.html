Trading Notes & Edge Theories
15-Min Delayed Data Cascade
TradingView 15-min delayed data creates sequential buyer waves. First leg forms on real-time buyers, second and third legs follow as delayed data catches up. Early entry on first leg is critical before cascade accelerates.
AI Amplification Theory
Human discovery precedes algorithmic pile-in. Retail finds setups first, algos detect and amplify the move. The gap between discovery and algo recognition is our edge — first movers capture the highest ROI before liquidity dries.
Movement Creates Movement
Each price leg feeds the next. Strong momentum off support creates fresh demand, which sustains the move. Breakout volume confirms the sequence; fading volume warns of reversal. Read legs as chained events, not isolated candles.
Dividend Rotation
Capital rotates into dividend payers before ex-dates. Watch yield spikes and ex-date calendars — predictable rotation creates low-float, high-catalyst setups. Post-ex, capital often rotates to growth, creating secondary moves.
Float Analysis Edge
Tight floats amplify moves. Float ≤20M + rel vol >1.5x + catalyst = explosive setups. The fewer shares available, the less volume needed to move price. Pair tight float with institutional catalyst (earnings, FDA, short squeeze catalyst).
VWAP as Dynamic S/R
VWAP isn't static — it reprices with each bar. Price holding above VWAP = accumulation phase. Break below = distribution warning. Entry above VWAP on breakout = confirmation of buyer conviction. Exit signals when price closes below VWAP on volume.
