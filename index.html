Dividend Capture Strategy

Buy before ex-dividend date, collect dividend, sell after. Net gain = dividend minus price slippage on exit.

  • Enter 1-2 days before ex-dividend date
  • Collect the dividend
  • Exit shortly after — stock typically drops by dividend amount on ex-date
  • Select large cap, stable dividend payers with low volatility

The Delayed Data Cascade Theory

Real-time traders see the spike first and act. 15-min delayed traders see it later and act — creating a second spike. Each wave of delayed info = new wave of buyers.

The edge: real-time data puts you in before the delayed crowd even knows the move started. The delayed crowd becomes your exit liquidity.

Core Theory — Movement Creates Movement

If a stock can go from $1 to $2, it can double again to $5. Each leg feeds the next. The only factors that stop it are supply, sentiment shift, or catalyst exhaustion.

Ride the momentum, cut quickly if it turns. You can't win if you're not in the trade.

Short Squeeze Mechanics

Low float + rising price + shorts underwater = forced covering = more upside. The lower the float the more violent the squeeze.

  • Stock spikes on catalyst → shorts pile in thinking it's topping
  • Shorts forced to cover → adds fuel → squeeze intensifies
  • Repeat until actual top
